## 2. Who We Serve

### Primary ICP — Logistics & Trucking Companies Hiring Lease-On Owner-Operators

Our core customer is a **dispatch authority / motor carrier** that wants to scale its fleet *asset-light* — meaning they don't want to buy more trucks; they want more independent owner-operators running freight under their authority.

**Why this matters (industry context):**
A lease-on owner-operator is an independent contractor who owns their own truck (and sometimes trailer) but operates under the motor carrier's MC/DOT authority. The carrier handles dispatch, freight, compliance, and back-office; the owner-operator gets steady loads without having to build a brokerage relationship from scratch. The carrier earns a percentage of the load revenue (typically 10–25%), and grows revenue *without* buying equipment, hiring W-2 drivers, or taking on financing risk.

The problem: **finding lease-on owner-operators is brutally hard.**
- Job boards are saturated with low-intent applicants and "ghost" leads.
- Recruiting agencies charge $750+ per signed driver, eating margins.
- The best owner-operators aren't browsing job boards — they're already running, often hitting cargo broker walls, fighting insurance renewals, or coming off out-of-service status.

Asamblor reaches that "invisible" market directly.

### Secondary ICP

- **Trucking companies hiring company drivers (CDL-A / W-2)** — same outreach engine, different audience filters.
- **Service providers to the trucking industry** — factoring companies, insurance brokers, leasing companies, tire dealers, fuel programs, ELD vendors, and anyone else already using carriex.io to source carrier data. Asamblor turns that data into booked meetings and quote requests.

## 3. The Problem We Solve

Traditional fleet growth in trucking is broken in three ways:

1. **The Lead Board Paradox** — public job boards generate volume, not quality. Recruiters waste hours chasing applicants who never respond, already signed elsewhere, or never had the equipment they claimed.
2. **The Agency Trap** — flat $750+ per-hire fees turn every scaling decision into a margin question. Scale up, and recruiting costs spiral faster than revenue.
3. **The Broker Wall** — thousands of qualified owner-operators are right now hitting capacity, insurance, or freight-access walls. They *want* a lease-on partnership. But internal recruiters have no scalable way to reach them.

Asamblor closes all three gaps with one integrated engine.

## 4. How Asamblor Works — The Engine

Our delivery model is a fully integrated, automated workflow that runs 24/5 to find, vet, and deliver qualified owner-operators (or drivers, or carriers) to the client's desk.

### Stage 1 — Onboarding & Targeting
We interview the client to capture the exact ICP:
- Authority type, lanes, trailer types pulled, freight classes
- Geographic targeting (state, region, radius)
- Fleet-size filters (1–2 truck owners vs. small fleets vs. mid-sized)
- Authority age, insurance posture, out-of-service status, inactive authorities
- Disqualifiers (e.g. hazmat-only, reefer-only, sub-2-year MC, etc.)

### Stage 2 — Data Mining via CarrieX
We use **carriex.io**, our proprietary data platform, to mine **4.5M+ verified trucking records**. The output is a clean, deduped, verified list of carriers and owner-operators that match the ICP exactly — including verified emails and phone numbers, authority status, and equipment data.

This is the step that separates us from every other agency: **we don't buy lead lists. We own the data layer.**

### Stage 3 — Outbound Infrastructure Build
We provision the full sending stack on the client's behalf:
- New sending domains, warmed mailboxes, SPF / DKIM / DMARC configured properly
- Telephony infrastructure for SMS follow-ups (10DLC / toll-free registered per jurisdiction)
- Custom vetting forms that capture trailer type, authority number, experience, equipment, and background
- A dedicated CRM pipeline built around the client's actual sales/recruiting process

### Stage 4 — Multi-Channel Engagement
Once live, the engine runs continuously:

- **Automated email outreach** — high-deliverability sequences sending up to 75,000 targeted emails per month with personalization, A/B testing, reply detection, and inbox rotation
- **SMS follow-ups** — when a lead replies to an email or clicks a link, an automated SMS fires to their phone within minutes, dramatically lifting reply rates. Fully TCPA-compliant (only fires on explicit interest signal)
- **Web vetting forms** — interested leads land on a dynamic form that pre-qualifies them before they ever touch a recruiter
- **Telephony routing** — qualified leads can be routed straight to a calling queue or human recruiter

### Stage 5 — CRM Pipeline & Automation
Every qualified lead lands in the client's CRM (we build and manage the pipeline for them) with full context: source, campaign, last touch, vetting form answers, equipment data. Automations handle:
- Stage transitions (new → contacted → vetted → signed → onboarded)
- Drag-and-drop triggers (move a card → fire a workflow in the background)
- "No response in N days" re-engagement sequences
- Lead routing by territory, trailer type, or recruiter capacity

### Stage 6 — Real-Time Team Notifications
Internal notification bots ping the right person the moment something happens:
- New hot lead → client's recruiting team
- Vetting form completed → recruiter's phone
- Campaign anomaly → our ops team

No qualified driver ever falls through the cracks because nobody saw the email.

### Stage 7 — Command Center & Reporting
The client gets a dashboard view of the entire pipeline:
- Emails sent, deliverability, opens, replies
- Inbound leads by stage
- Vetting form completions
- Signed drivers per campaign
- Cost per lead and cost per hire

Full transparency on where every dollar of outreach spend is going.
